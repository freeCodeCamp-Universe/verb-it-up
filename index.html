📚 Grammar note

A phrasal verb is a verb combined with a particle (preposition or adverb) that together carry a new meaning, often unrelated to the individual words.

  • Separable: the object can go between the verb and particle:
    turn off the TV → turn the TV off
  • Inseparable: the object must follow the full phrasal verb:
    come across an old friend ✓
    come an old friend across ✗
  • Intransitive: some take no object at all:
    The engine broke down.
